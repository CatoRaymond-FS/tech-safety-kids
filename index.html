Tech & Internet Safety for Kids

Learn how to use technology safely and responsibly!

What is a Computer?

Computers are tools that help us do many things like writing, drawing, and even playing games. They have parts that work together to make everything run smoothly!

Basic Parts:

  • Monitor: This is the screen where you see everything you do.
  • Keyboard: This is where you type words and numbers.
  • Mouse: This helps you point, click, and move around the screen.
  • CPU (Central Processing Unit): Think of it as the "brain" of the computer that makes everything work.

Each part has a special job, and they all work together to let you use the computer for fun and learning!

Getting Around the Screen

Now that you know about the computer parts, let's talk about how to use them!

Using the Mouse and Keyboard:

  • Left Click: Click on things you want to open or select.
  • Right Click: This opens a menu with more choices.
  • Scroll Wheel: This lets you move up and down on pages.
  • Typing: Use the keyboard to type letters, numbers, and symbols.

Understanding the Desktop, Files, and Folders:

The desktop is the main screen where you see all your icons. These icons are like shortcuts to programs and files.

Files are where you keep your work or documents, and folders help you organize them so they don’t get lost.

Tip: It's important to keep your schoolwork organized in folders so you can find it easily later!

The Internet: What Is It?

The internet is like a big network that connects everyone around the world. It helps us talk to people, find information, and play games!

What the Internet Does:

  • Connects people through email, messaging, and social media.
  • Helps us find information on any topic—like school subjects, hobbies, or fun facts!
  • Allows us to play games, watch videos, and have fun.

What is a Browser?

A browser is the program you use to explore the internet. Popular browsers include Google Chrome, Safari, and Firefox. It's like your "vehicle" to travel around the internet!

Safe Internet Use: Always use kid-friendly search engines and websites. Ask an adult if you're unsure about any website you want to visit.

Internet Safety 101

It's important to stay safe while you're online. Here are some simple tips:

Why Safety Matters:

When you're online, you can meet people and see things you don’t expect. It's important to make sure you're safe from things like strangers, viruses, and scams.

Tips for Staying Safe:

  • Use strong passwords and don’t share them with anyone.
  • Don’t share your personal information like your name, address, or phone number.
  • Always ask an adult before clicking on links or opening attachments in emails.

Safe Websites vs. Suspicious Websites:

Make sure the websites you visit are safe! Look for “https” at the beginning of the URL and a little lock icon in the address bar. These show that the website is secure.

Online Etiquette

Being respectful online is just as important as being respectful in person!

Being Kind:

Always be polite when chatting with others, whether you're in games, on social media, or in class forums.

Don’t Be a Bully:

If you see someone being rude or bullying others, let an adult know. It’s important to stand up for others and report bad behavior.

Fun & Learning

Technology isn't just for entertainment—it can help you learn new things too!

Safe Educational Games and Websites:

  • Scratch: A fun way to learn coding by creating your own stories, games, and animations.
  • Cool Math Games: A great place to play games that also help you practice math!
  • Khan Academy: Learn new things in subjects like math, science, and art through videos and quizzes.

Ways to Explore Tech:

Try out drawing tools like Sketchpad, or typing games to improve your typing speed. Learn the basics of coding on Scratch.

Ask for Help!

If something ever confuses you, it’s important to ask for help. Talking to a trusted adult can make things easier to understand.

Tech Trouble Checklist:

  • Is your device not turning on? Try holding the power button for a few seconds.
  • Is your internet slow? Try checking if other devices are using it or ask for help from an adult.
  • If a website doesn’t look right or makes you feel uncomfortable, stop using it and ask an adult.

Remember, there’s always someone who can help if you need it!
